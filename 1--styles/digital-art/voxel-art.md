# Style Profile: Voxel Art
**Source:** Adobe Creative Cloud / Illustration / Digital Art
**Category:** 3D Digital Art / Retro-Stylized Graphics
**Database ID:** VXL-001

## 1. Technical Definition
Voxel Art is the creation of digital images using **colored 3D boxes (voxels)**. 
- **Core Unit:** The "Voxel" (a portmanteau of "volume" and "element" or "pixel").
- **Structure:** Voxels are arranged on a 3D grid to create recognizable shapes.
- **Complexity Scaling:** The level of detail is directly proportional to the "voxel density." 
    - *Low-density:* 10-50 voxels (crude/abstract).
    - *High-density:* Thousands of voxels (highly detailed replicas).
- **Artistic Parallel:** Often compared to **Pointillism** (dots in 2D) or **LEGO** (physical 3D blocks).

## 2. Voxel vs. Pixel (The 2D/3D Divide)
For AI prompting and style classification, distinguishing between these is critical:
- **Pixels:** 2D squares, flat, must be applied to a pre-existing shape/surface to give color.
- **Voxels:** 3D cubes, occupy physical volume in a digital space, self-supporting structure.

## 3. Style Evolution & History
- **The Golden Age:** Heavily used in 1990s video game production due to ease of rendering 3D shapes.
- **The Decline:** Fell out of the mainstream as hardware evolved to favor polygon-based rendering and complex meshes.
- **The Modern Renaissance:** Now used primarily for "Retro-Chic" aesthetics or specific procedural generation engines.

## 4. Key Reference Works & Games
If training or prompting an AI, these are the primary "gold standard" references:
- **Minecraft:** The most famous utilization of large-scale voxel environments.
- **No Man's Sky:** Procedural terrain and planetary generation.
- **Crossy Road:** Example of "Voxel-Pop" aesthetic (bright, clean, simplified).
- **Starbase:** Complex mechanical voxel construction.

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions (Raw Extraction)
- **Is Voxel Art Easy?** It is simple to start due to the grid-based undo/redo nature, but large-scale scenes require significant technical know-how.
- **Why don't all games use it?** AAA games shifted to polygons for more realistic "smoothing." Voxels remain a distinct, deliberate "stylistic choice" rather than a technical limitation.
